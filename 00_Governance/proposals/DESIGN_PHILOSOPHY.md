## The 5-Year Journey

**Creator:** Chad
**Time:** 5 years of development and hard times
**Result:** Revolutionary soap-keeping device using gimbal elevation

**The Innovation:**

- Frame gimbals in hull sidewall slots (ball-and-socket pivot)
- Swing limited to <45° by structural hull property
- NO drain holes - water drips below, evaporates via airflow
- Soap stays dry, lasts longer

## Hull Design Philosophy

**The Hull is the Stator:**
- Connects two frames to their positions
- Sidewall slots enable gimbal pivot
- Wall thickness must house slots AND maintain structure
- Some structural property limits swing to <45°
- Floor collects water (no drain holes)
- Open top enables airflow

**Hull Design is NOT about:**
- ❌ Replicating canoe hull forms
- ❌ Naval architecture accuracy
- ❌ Traditional boat construction

**Hull Design IS about:**
- ✅ Supporting the gimbal mechanism
- ✅ Housing frame pivot slots structurally
- ✅ Limiting frame swing symmetrically
- ✅ Enabling the elevation system

---

## Frame Design Philosophy

**The Frames are the Rotor:**
- Inverse trapezoid (narrower bottom, wider top)
- Ball joints extend laterally into hull slots
- Top members have edge geometry (flat→90° bite)
- Weight distribution creates self-righting bias
- Gimbal action creates frictional soap grip

**Frame Design is NOT about:**
- ❌ Canoe seat aesthetics
- ❌ Decorative elements
- ❌ Over-engineering

**Frame Design IS about:**
- ✅ Elevating soap above hull floor
- ✅ Creating gripping bite through gimbal + edge geometry
- ✅ Minimal line contact (not surface contact)
- ✅ Enabling controlled swing within <45° limit

## Research Focus Areas

**When researching, prioritize:**

1. **Gimbal Mechanics**
   - Slot-and-ball pivot geometry
   - Concave slot floor for smooth rotation
   - Hull wall thickness vs. slot depth trade-offs
   - Swing limitation mechanisms

2. **OpenSCAD Best Practices**
   - Parametric CSG approaches
   - BOSL2 library capabilities
   - Modular geometry generation
   - Maintainable code structure

3. **Soap Contact Optimization**
   - Edge geometry for gripping (flat→90° transition)
   - Minimal contact area (line, not surface)
   - Frame spacing for standard soap bars

4. **Structural Requirements**
   - Hull wall thickness for slot housing
   - Frame dimensionality for proper swing
   - Pivot robustness (repeated loading)

**NOT research priorities:**
- Canoe hull form accuracy
- Traditional boat construction
- Naval architecture beyond basic terminology

## Decision-Making Framework

**When making design decisions, ask:**

1. **Does this preserve FR-0?** (soap elevation, gimbal, no drain holes)
   - If NO → Reject immediately
   - If YES → Proceed to next question

2. **Is this research-backed?** (not intuition, not arbitrary)
   - If NO → Do research first
   - If YES → Proceed to next question

3. **Does this serve the minimum complexity principle?** (Law 8)
   - If NO → Simplify
   - If YES → Implement

4. **Does this improve canoe aesthetics?**
   - Nice to have, but lowest priority
   - Never at expense of FR-0 or research principle

## Common Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**❌ "Let's make it more like a real canoe"**
- This is not a canoe. It's a soap device shaped like one.

**❌ "Traditional soap dishes have drain holes"**
- That's WHY this invention exists - to REPLACE drain holes.

**❌ "Flat top rails would be more comfortable"**
- Edge geometry creates the gripping bite. This is functional, not comfort.

**❌ "This is too complicated, let's simplify the gimbal"**
- The gimbal IS the invention. Simplify code, not mechanics.

**❌ "Users might not understand the mechanism"**
- Our job: make it work. User's job: put soap on it. It works.
